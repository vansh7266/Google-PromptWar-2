Step by Step

India's Election Timeline

From announcement to results — every phase explained clearly.

📢
Phase 1

Election Announcement

The Election Commission of India (ECI) issues the official election schedule, triggering the Model Code of Conduct (MCC). Political parties must follow strict rules from this point.

  • ECI announces dates via press conference
  • MCC comes into immediate effect
  • Government expenditure and transfers are restricted
📋
Phase 2

Voter Registration

Citizens above 18 years can register through the NVSP portal or Form 6. Your name must appear on the Electoral Roll to vote.

  • Visit nvsp.in or use Voter Helpline 1950
  • Submit Form 6 for new registration
  • Check name via CEO's website of your state
🙋
Phase 3

Candidate Nomination

Candidates file their nomination papers with the Returning Officer. Scrutiny follows, and candidates can withdraw within the stipulated deadline.

  • Nomination papers filed with supporting signatures
  • Scrutiny by Returning Officer
  • Last date for withdrawal of candidature
📣
Phase 4

Election Campaign

Political parties and candidates campaign across constituencies. Campaigning must stop 48 hours before polling (the "campaign silence period").

  • Rallies, roadshows, door-to-door campaigns
  • Expenditure limits enforced by ECI
  • Campaign silence 48 hrs before voting
🗳️
Phase 5

Polling Day

Voters cast their votes using Electronic Voting Machines (EVM). VVPAT slips confirm the vote. Polls generally run from 7 AM to 6 PM.

  • Carry valid Voter ID / Aadhaar / listed documents
  • Press the EVM button for your candidate
  • VVPAT slip appears for 7 seconds for verification
🔢
Phase 6

Vote Counting & Results

Votes are counted on a designated date under strict supervision. Results are declared constituency by constituency and the winning candidate is notified.

  • Counting starts at 8 AM on counting day
  • Agents of candidates observe the process
  • Candidate with most votes wins (First Past the Post)
